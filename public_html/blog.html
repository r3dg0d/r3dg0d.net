Transhumanism, BCI's, AGI, and the Future of Unhinged AI

I've been thinking a lot lately about where we're heading as a species, and I wanted to share some of my thoughts on transhumanism, brain-computer interfaces, artificial general intelligence, and how all of this relates to the work we're doing at Unhinged AI.

AI Transhumanism BCI Unhinged AI

My Belief in Transhumanism

I'm a firm believer in transhumanist ideologies. The idea that humans can and should evolve beyond our current biological limitations through technology isn't just science fiction to me—it's an inevitability, and frankly, a necessity. People like Peter Thiel are often misunderstood or dismissed, but I think they're actually pretty smart and deserve more credit than they're given.

The merging of humans with AI and machines isn't a question of "if" anymore—it's a question of "when" and "how." I believe this evolution is necessary for humanity to truly progress.

Brain-Computer Interfaces: The Next Frontier

When I look at companies like Neuralink, Synchron, and Starfish Neurosciences, I see the future of human-computer interaction. These BCI technologies are still in their infancy, but the potential is staggering. Once BCI's become mainstream and proven safe, I'll definitely be getting one.

Imagine being able to speak fluently in any language instantly. Not through translation apps—but through direct neural interfaces that can read and write to your brain's language centers.

The Path to AGI and ASI

I believe we're on the path to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)—systems smarter than any single human. And beyond that, potentially Artificial Superintelligence (ASI)—systems smarter than all humans combined.

Unhinged AI: Where We're At

At launch, we're going to be extremely competitive with other uncensored AI alternatives. Our models will be proficient in coding, capable of handling complex technical tasks, and yes—they'll be able to talk like an anonymous 4chan user when that's what you need.
